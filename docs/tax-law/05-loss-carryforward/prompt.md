# Research: Crypto Loss Carry-Forward Rules in Poland

## Context

A Polish taxpayer has crypto trading losses in some years and gains in others. Based on our current calculations:

- 2020: Loss of ~461 PLN (but taxpayer was in Sweden -- may not apply)
- 2021: Net gain ~31,000 PLN (Sweden)
- 2022: Net gain ~150,000 PLN (Sweden, but became Polish resident ~2023)
- 2023: Net gain ~146,000 PLN (Poland, first full year)
- 2024: Net gain ~3,000 PLN (Poland)
- 2025: Loss of ~15,000 PLN (Poland)

The losses in 2025 should be available for carry-forward to 2026-2030. Additionally, if previous years are corrected and show losses (e.g., if cost basis was previously underestimated), those losses could matter too.

## Questions

### Basic Loss Rules
1. Under Art. 9 ust. 3 ustawy o PIT, a tax loss (strata) can be carried forward for 5 years. What are the exact rules?
   - Is the maximum deduction 50% of the loss per year? (Art. 9 ust. 3 pkt 1)
   - OR can up to 5,000,000 PLN be deducted in one year? (Art. 9 ust. 3 pkt 2 -- newer rule)
   - Which of these applies to crypto losses specifically?

2. Do crypto losses (from PIT-38) only offset crypto gains? Or can they offset other capital gains (stocks, bonds)?

3. Can crypto losses offset business income (ryczalt/JDG)? Or are they completely separate?

4. If the taxpayer has a loss in 2025 on PIT-38, what exactly appears on the form? How is the loss formally declared?

### Cross-Border Loss Issues
5. If losses were incurred while the taxpayer was a Swedish tax resident (2020-2022), can those losses be carried forward in Poland?
6. If the taxpayer filed a crypto tax return in Sweden showing losses, can those same losses also be claimed in Poland? Or would that be double-counting?
7. What if the taxpayer was a Polish tax resident starting mid-2023 -- can losses from the second half of 2023 onwards be carried forward?

### Practical Application
8. If the 2025 loss is 15,000 PLN, how much can be deducted in 2026?
   - Under the 50% rule: max 7,500 PLN per year?
   - Under the 5M PLN rule: the full 15,000 PLN in 2026?
9. Must the loss carry-forward be declared in the year of the loss, or can it be claimed retroactively when filing the future year?
10. If a corrected (korekta) PIT-38 for a prior year reveals a loss that wasn't originally declared, can that loss be carried forward?

### Documentation
11. What documentation is needed to support a loss carry-forward claim?
12. Must the original loss year's PIT-38 be filed (or corrected) before the loss can be carried forward?

## Desired Output

Exact rules for crypto loss carry-forward with:
- Legal references (Art. 9 ust. 3 ustawy o PIT and any crypto-specific provisions)
- Practical examples showing year-by-year calculation
- Cross-border considerations for Sweden-to-Poland transition
- Instructions for declaring losses on PIT-38

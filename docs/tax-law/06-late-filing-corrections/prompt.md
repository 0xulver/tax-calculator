# Research: Late Filing and Corrections for PIT-38 in Poland

## Context

A Polish tax resident became resident around 2023 (previously Sweden). They did NOT file PIT-38 (crypto tax) for 2023 or 2024. The deadline for PIT-38 2023 was April 30, 2024. The deadline for PIT-38 2024 was April 30, 2025. Both deadlines have passed (current date: March 2026).

The taxpayer had crypto disposals (selling crypto for EUR) in both years:
- 2023: ~146,000 PLN net income from crypto disposals
- 2024: ~3,000 PLN net income from crypto disposals (plus newly discovered "Sell Crypto To Fiat" events that were previously not tracked)

The taxpayer wants to voluntarily come into compliance. They may also need to file corrected declarations if they filed a PIT-36/PIT-37 but omitted PIT-38.

## Questions

### Filing Late PIT-38
1. Can a PIT-38 be filed after the deadline? What is the procedure?
2. Is filing a late PIT-38 the same as filing a "korekta" (correction), or is it a first-time filing?
3. Must the late filing be accompanied by a "czynny zal" (voluntary disclosure / active remorse letter) to avoid penalties?
4. What exactly is a "czynny zal" and what must it contain? Is there a template or specific requirements (Art. 16 Kodeks karny skarbowy)?
5. Can the czynny zal and late PIT-38 be filed electronically through e-Urzad Skarbowy, or must it be done in person or by mail?

### Penalties and Interest
6. What penalties apply for late filing of PIT-38?
   - Kara grzywny (fine) -- what range?
   - Odsetki za zwloke (late payment interest) -- what rate? How calculated?
7. Does the czynny zal eliminate penalties entirely, or only reduce them?
8. Is there a statute of limitations for tax obligations? Can the tax office go back more than 5 years?
9. If the taxpayer files voluntarily before any audit or inquiry, does that improve their position?

### Tax Due and Payment
10. If PIT-38 for 2023 shows 27,000 PLN tax due (19% of ~146,000 PLN), how and when must this be paid?
11. Are late payment interest (odsetki) calculated from the original deadline (April 30, 2024) to the payment date?
12. What is the current interest rate for late tax payment in Poland?
13. Can the taxpayer request installment payments (raty) if the total amount is large?

### Corrections to Previously Filed Returns
14. If the taxpayer filed PIT-36 or PIT-37 for 2023 but did not file PIT-38, is the PIT-38 treated as a new filing or a correction?
15. If corrections are needed to both PIT-36/37 AND PIT-38 for the same year, how does that work?
16. How many corrections can be filed? Is there a limit?
17. What is the deadline for filing corrections? Is it 5 years from the end of the tax year?

### Practical Steps
18. What is the recommended order of operations:
    a) File czynny zal first, then PIT-38?
    b) File PIT-38 and czynny zal simultaneously?
    c) Pay the tax first, then file?
19. Can a tax advisor (doradca podatkowy) handle this process?
20. Is it advisable to consult with the local tax office (urzad skarbowy) before filing, or just file and pay?

## Desired Output

A step-by-step guide for voluntarily coming into compliance with late PIT-38 filings in Poland, including:
- Exact legal basis for czynny zal (Kodeks karny skarbowy)
- Template or requirements for the czynny zal letter
- Interest calculation methodology
- Recommended sequence of actions
- Risk assessment (likelihood and severity of penalties with vs without czynny zal)

# PIT-38 Late Filing & Voluntary Compliance Guide — Poland

## Executive Summary

A Polish tax resident who missed PIT-38 deadlines for 2023 (due April 30, 2024) and 2024 (due April 30, 2025) can still come into voluntary compliance. The process involves filing corrected declarations, paying overdue tax with accrued interest, and submitting a *czynny żal* (voluntary disclosure under Art. 16 KKS). Acting before any tax office inquiry or audit prevents criminal-fiscal penalties almost entirely. For the scenario described (approx. 27,740 PLN tax for 2023 and ~570 PLN for 2024), total liability as of mid-March 2026 is approximately **35,373 PLN** including interest — but zero in fines if voluntary disclosure is handled correctly.

***

## Part 1 — Can PIT-38 Still Be Filed? What Is the Procedure?

### Late Filing Is Always Permitted

Yes — a PIT-38 can be filed after the statutory deadline at any time up to the tax statute of limitations. There is no hard cutoff that bars late filing. Polish tax regulations explicitly allow submission of "outstanding returns" (*zaległe zeznanie*) on the form in effect for the tax year in question.[^1][^2]

### First Filing vs. Korekta (Correction)

This distinction is critically important and has a practical twist for 2024:

- **PIT-38 for 2023:** The service *Twój e-PIT* automatically accepted PIT-37 and PIT-38 returns if the taxpayer took no action by April 30, 2024. If the taxpayer was registered in the Polish system and had prior-year data, a PIT-38 **may have been auto-accepted with zero crypto income**. Any subsequent filing is therefore treated as a **korekta** (correction).[^3][^4][^5][^6]
- **PIT-38 for 2024:** Confirmed — *Twój e-PIT* automatically accepted PIT-38 for 2024 on May 1–8, 2025. Any filing after that date is formally a **korekta**, regardless of whether the taxpayer interacted with the system.[^7][^8][^3]

**Practical implication:** Before filing, log into *e-Urząd Skarbowy* (podatki.gov.pl) and check whether a PIT-38 was auto-accepted for 2023 and 2024. If so, mark the new filing as "2. korekta zeznania" in Part A, field 6 of the form.[^9]

> ⚠️ **Important caveat:** If the taxpayer was a new resident in 2023 and had no prior-year data in the Polish system, the auto-acceptance may not have generated a PIT-38. In that case, the late filing could be treated as a first filing. Verify by checking the "Złożone dokumenty" section in *e-Urząd Skarbowy*.

## Part 2 — Czynny Żal: Legal Basis, Contents, and Procedure

### What Is Czynny Żal?

*Czynny żal* is a voluntary disclosure mechanism regulated by **Art. 16 of the Kodeks karny skarbowy (KKS)**. It is a written notification to the tax authority in which the taxpayer admits to committing a prohibited act (in this case, failing to file PIT-38 and failing to pay the resulting tax on time). When validly submitted **before** the tax office initiates proceedings, it results in the taxpayer not being subject to criminal-fiscal penalties.[^10][^11][^12][^13]

There is also a **"small" czynny żal** under **Art. 16a KKS**, introduced in its current form in 2005 and amended as of January 1, 2022. Under this provision, simply filing a valid correction of the declaration and paying the overdue tax promptly is sufficient to escape criminal-fiscal liability — no separate disclosure letter is required. However, **after the 2022 Polish Ład amendments**, Art. 16a no longer guarantees immunity if proceedings have already been initiated, and in practice many tax advisors recommend filing a full Art. 16 czynny żal alongside the correction to maximize protection.[^14][^15][^16][^17][^18]

### When Czynny Żal Is Ineffective

Czynny żal is **void** if any of the following have occurred before submission:[^11][^13][^17]

- The tax office has already initiated an investigation or preparatory proceedings (postępowanie przygotowawcze)
- A tax audit (kontrola podatkowa) notification has been sent
- The taxpayer has already been caught during ongoing proceedings

This underscores the urgency of acting now, before any contact from the tax authority.

### Required Contents of the Czynny Żal Letter

Under Art. 16 § 1 KKS, the notice must:[^19][^20][^10]

1. **Personal identification data** — full name, PESEL/NIP, address
2. **Addressee** — Naczelnik właściwego Urzędu Skarbowego (head of the competent tax office)
3. **Description of the prohibited act** — specifically, the failure to file PIT-38 for tax year 2023 (deadline April 30, 2024) and for tax year 2024 (deadline April 30, 2025), and the resulting failure to pay the tax due on time
4. **Brief justification** — explanation of why the obligation was not met (e.g., lack of awareness of Polish tax obligations as a new resident, administrative oversight). A simple truthful statement is sufficient[^13]
5. **Declaration of no co-perpetrators** — a statement confirming no other persons were involved in the offense (required under Art. 16 § 1 KKS)[^21]
6. **Declaration of remediation** — a statement that the taxpayer has already filed the overdue declarations and paid (or will pay) all overdue tax and interest by a specific date[^20][^19]
7. **Date and signature**

The letter does **not** need to cite specific KKS article numbers — a clear factual description of the offense is legally sufficient.[^13]

### How to Submit — Electronic Procedure

Czynny żal can be submitted electronically and does **not** require an in-person visit:[^22][^23]

1. Log in to **e-Urząd Skarbowy** (eurzad.skarbowy.gov.pl) using Profil Zaufany or e-Dowód
2. Select **"Złóż pismo ogólne do urzędu"** (Submit general letter to the office)
3. Paste or attach the czynny żal text
4. Attach the filed corrections (UPO confirmations)
5. Submit and **download and save the UPO** (Urzędowe Poświadczenie Odbioru) as proof of filing[^23]

Alternatively, it can be sent via **ePUAP** to the competent tax office's mailbox, or submitted in writing by registered post, or in person. All electronic submissions must be signed with *kwalifikowany podpis elektroniczny*, *podpis zaufany*, or *podpis osobisty*.[^24][^25][^10]

## Part 3 — Penalties: What Applies and How to Avoid Them

### Classification of the Offense

The classification of the offense determines the severity of potential penalties:[^26]

| Year | Tax Due | 5× Minimum Wage Threshold (approx.) | Classification |
|------|---------|--------------------------------------|----------------|
| 2023 | ~27,740 PLN | ~18,000–21,525 PLN | **Przestępstwo skarbowe** (fiscal crime) |
| 2024 | ~570 PLN | ~21,525–26,400 PLN | **Wykroczenie skarbowe** (fiscal misdemeanor) |

The 2023 liability likely falls into the *przestępstwo skarbowe* category because the underpaid tax exceeds 5× the minimum wage threshold applicable at the time.[^26]

### Potential Penalties Without Czynny Żal

**Wykroczenie skarbowe (2024 year):**
- Fine: from 1/10 to 20× the minimum wage[^26]
- Approximately 430 PLN (minimum) to 86,000 PLN (maximum) based on 2024 wages

**Przestępstwo skarbowe (2023 year) — Art. 54 KKS:**
- Fine: up to 720 daily rate-units (*stawki dzienne*), or imprisonment, or both[^27]
- Maximum fine can theoretically reach tens of millions of PLN for severe cases[^10]
- In practice, for voluntary first-time disclosure of moderate amounts, fines are at the lower end

**With valid czynny żal: zero criminal-fiscal penalty in either case**.[^12][^11]

### Criminal Statute of Limitations

- Wykroczenia skarbowe: criminal proceedings expire 3 years after commission
- Przestępstwa skarbowe (imprisonment ≤3 years): expire **5 years** after commission[^26]

The 2023 offense (non-filing) occurred on April 30, 2024 — the criminal statute runs until approximately April 30, 2029. This creates urgency: czynny żal must be filed well before that date, and ideally immediately.

## Part 4 — Tax Liability, Interest, and the 5-Year Statute of Limitations

### Tax Statute of Limitations

Under **Art. 70 § 1 of the Ordynacja Podatkowa**, tax obligations expire after **5 years from the end of the calendar year in which the tax payment deadline fell**:[^28][^29][^30]

| Tax Year | Payment Deadline | Statute Expires |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 2023 | April 30, 2024 | December 31, 2029 |
| 2024 | April 30, 2025 | December 31, 2030 |

The tax office can issue assessments and demand payment within these windows. After expiry, the obligation extinguishes by operation of law. Both years are well within the statute.

### Interest on Overdue Tax (Odsetki za Zwłokę)

Interest accrues from the day **after** the original payment deadline under **Art. 53 § 1 of the Ordynacja Podatkowa**. The rate is set by formula under **Art. 56 § 1 OP**: 200% of the NBP Lombard rate + 2% (minimum 8%). The taxpayer must self-calculate and pay interest without awaiting a demand.[^31][^32][^33]

### Can the Reduced Rate (50%) Apply?

**No, for either year.** Under **Art. 56a § 1 of the Ordynacja Podatkowa**, the 50% reduced rate requires:[^39][^40]
1. A valid correction filed **within 6 months** of the original filing deadline
2. Full tax payment within 7 days of filing the correction

The 6-month windows expired on October 31, 2024 (for 2023) and October 31, 2025 (for 2024). Both deadlines have passed, so the full standard rate applies throughout.[^40][^39]

### Installment Payments (Raty)

If the total amount (~35,373 PLN) presents a cash-flow challenge, a taxpayer can request installment payment under **Art. 67a § 1 of the Ordynacja Podatkowa**. Key points:[^41][^42]

- The request must demonstrate "ważny interes podatnika" (important taxpayer interest) or "interes publiczny" (public interest)[^42][^43]
- The decision is **discretionary** — the tax office evaluates the taxpayer's financial and personal situation[^44][^42]
- The request is filed using the **RAT-Z form**, or as a free-form letter, and can be submitted electronically[^41][^42]
- Supporting documents: income tax returns, bank statements, evidence of financial hardship
- During an approved installment arrangement, reduced interest (*opłata prolongacyjna*) may apply instead of standard late interest

## Part 5 — Corrections to Other Declarations (PIT-36/PIT-37)

### Filing PIT-38 Alongside Other PITs

If the taxpayer filed a PIT-36 or PIT-37 for 2023 or 2024, the PIT-38 is an entirely **separate declaration** covering a distinct income category (capital gains from securities and crypto). Crypto income is reported in its own section of PIT-38 and does not combine with other income types. Therefore:[^2][^45][^46]

- The PIT-38 is **not a correction of PIT-36/PIT-37** — they are separate forms
- Both can be independently corrected without affecting the other
- If the taxpayer filed PIT-36/PIT-37 but simply never filed PIT-38, the PIT-38 is an outstanding return (or korekta of a zero auto-accepted one), not a correction of the other form

### Correction Limits and Deadlines

There is **no limit** on the number of corrections that can be filed. Corrections can be submitted at any time up to the statute of limitations (5 years from end of year of payment deadline). Each correction supersedes the prior filing. No written justification is required for voluntary corrections, unless filed during tax proceedings.[^29][^9]

## Part 6 — CRS Risk and Why Acting Now Matters

Poland participates in the **CRS (Common Reporting Standard)** automatic exchange of financial information. Financial institutions in most developed countries — including those operating crypto-to-EUR conversion platforms — report account holder information to their domestic tax authorities, who then share it with Polish tax authorities. This means:[^1]

- The Polish tax office may already have received data about the crypto-to-EUR transactions
- Large transfers of proceeds to bank accounts may trigger automated cross-matching
- Waiting increases the probability that the tax office discovers the omission first, at which point czynny żal becomes ineffective[^13]

## Part 7 — Recommended Sequence of Actions

The following sequence minimizes risk and maximizes the effectiveness of voluntary compliance:

### Step 1 — Verify Current Filing Status (Day 1)

Log into **e-Urząd Skarbowy** (eurzad.skarbowy.gov.pl) and check:
- Was a PIT-38 auto-accepted for 2023? For 2024? Check section "Złożone dokumenty"
- If yes, any new filing is a korekta (mark field 6 = "2. korekta zeznania")
- If no, it is an outstanding return (mark field 6 = "1. złożenie zeznania")

### Step 2 — Prepare Corrected PIT-38 Declarations

For each year (2023 and 2024):
- Use the **correct form version** for the applicable tax year (not the current year's form)
- For crypto: report gross revenue (*przychód*), deductible costs (*koszty*), and net income (*dochód*) in the dedicated crypto section of PIT-38[^47][^46]
- Apply **FIFO** cost methodology and PLN conversion using NBP average rates from the business day preceding each transaction[^1]
- If EUR transactions: convert to PLN using the NBP table rate from the day preceding the sale
- Consider engaging a *doradca podatkowy* (licensed tax advisor) for the crypto computation, especially for the 2024 "Sell Crypto To Fiat" events

### Step 3 — Calculate Interest Due

Calculate interest using the rate schedule above, or use the MF online calculator. Interest accrues from May 1 of the year following the tax year to the actual payment date. Self-assessment and payment without demand is standard practice.[^33]

### Step 4 — File the PIT-38 Corrections Electronically

Via *Twój e-PIT* or *e-Deklaracje* system. Ensure the form version matches the tax year. Download and save the **UPO** (Urzędowe Poświadczenie Odbioru) for each filed correction.[^48][^3][^7]

### Step 5 — Pay Tax + Interest Simultaneously

**On the same day as filing or the day before**, transfer the tax plus accrued interest to the tax office's bank account. The account number (microaccount: indywidualny rachunek podatkowy) can be found at:  
**podatki.gov.pl/generator-mikrorachunkow** — enter your PESEL.[^20]

It is strongly recommended to **pay before or simultaneously with filing** the czynny żal, not after. This demonstrates full remediation and reduces the likelihood of any follow-up.[^23][^13]

### Step 6 — File Czynny Żal via e-Urząd Skarbowy

**On the same day as (or before) filing the corrections:**

1. Go to e-Urząd Skarbowy → "Złóż pismo ogólne do urzędu"
2. Address it to the head of the competent tax office (właściwy urząd skarbowy, typically determined by place of residence)
3. Paste the czynny żal letter (use the template in Part 2)
4. Attach UPO confirmations of the filed corrections
5. Sign with Profil Zaufany
6. Download and preserve the UPO confirmation[^22][^23]

### Step 7 — Optional: Consult a Tax Advisor

A licensed *doradca podatkowy* can:
- Verify that the crypto computations are correct (especially FIFO, PLN conversion)
- Review the czynny żal letter before submission
- Represent the taxpayer before the tax office if any follow-up inquiry arises[^1]

Pre-filing consultation with the local tax office (urząd skarbowy) is generally **not necessary** and may actually be counterproductive — disclosing a problem verbally before formally filing czynny żal could be interpreted as initiating the process in a way that complicates the legal picture. The formal written submission is the correct channel.[^20]

## Part 8 — Risk Assessment

| Scenario | Penalty Risk | Interest | Total Cost |
|----------|-------------|----------|------------|
| **File now with czynny żal + pay immediately** | ~Zero (criminal immunity under Art. 16 KKS) | ~7,063 PLN (both years) | ~35,373 PLN |
| **File without czynny żal but before any audit** | Low (Art. 16a protection likely applies if correction valid) | ~7,063 PLN | ~35,373 PLN + potential fine |
| **Wait until tax office contacts taxpayer** | High — czynny żal ineffective; full criminal-fiscal proceedings possible | Interest + penalties + legal costs | Potentially 35,373+ PLN + fines up to 720 stawek dziennych |
| **Do nothing (statute expires)** | Criminal exposure until 2029–2030; audit risk active throughout | Interest continues accruing | Escalating liability |

The risk/reward calculus overwhelmingly favors immediate voluntary compliance. The 2023 liability is already above the *przestępstwo skarbowe* threshold, meaning the potential criminal consequences of inaction are significantly more serious than for the smaller 2024 amount.[^27][^26]

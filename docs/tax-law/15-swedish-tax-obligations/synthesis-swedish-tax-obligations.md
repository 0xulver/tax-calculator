# Synthesis: Swedish Tax Filing Obligations After Emigrating to Poland

Sources: ChatGPT 5.4 Pro, ChatGPT Deep Research, Perplexity, Gemini 3.1 Deep Research (4 sources)

## Full Consensus (All 4 agree)

### The taxpayer is begransat skattskyldig (limited tax liability) in Sweden

All 4 sources unanimously conclude that the taxpayer transitioned to begransat skattskyldig status on the date they left Sweden and established permanent residence in Poland in early 2023. The analysis is straightforward: the taxpayer meets none of the three grounds for obegransat skattskyldighet under IL 3 kap. 3 section -- they are not domiciled in Sweden, do not habitually stay there, and lack vasentlig anknytning.

**Legal basis**: Inkomstskattelagen (1999:1229), 3 kap. 3 section

### Vasentlig anknytning does NOT exist on these facts

All 4 sources analyze IL 3 kap. 7 section and reach the same conclusion: the taxpayer has comprehensively severed all material connection points.

| Connection Factor (IL 3 kap. 7 section) | Taxpayer Status | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Year-round dwelling in Sweden | None | Strongly favors limited liability (heaviest factor) |
| Family (spouse/minor children) in Sweden | None | Strongly favors limited liability (heaviest factor) |
| Business activity in Sweden | None (JDG is Polish) | Strongly favors limited liability |
| Economic engagement / influence in Swedish business | None | Strongly favors limited liability |
| Swedish real estate | None | Strongly favors limited liability |
| Swedish citizenship | Retained | Weak factor; triggers 5-year presumption but NOT decisive |
| Permanently settled abroad | Yes (Poland) | Strongly favors limited liability |
| Swedish bank account | Possibly retained | NOT a statutory factor; passive capital placement |
| Personnummer | Retained | NOT a factor at all -- administrative identifier only |

All sources cite case law confirming that **citizenship alone never creates vasentlig anknytning**. Skatterattsnamnden rulings (including a 2022-11-03 and 2026-01-30 ruling cited by ChatGPT 5.4 Pro) confirm that citizenship has "less significance" than substantive ties. A bank account is classified as a passive capital placement and is systematically disregarded (HFD 2012 ref 67, cited by Gemini). Personnummer is preserved after emigration and carries zero weight in the tax residency analysis.

### The 5-year presumption applies but is easily rebutted

All 4 sources confirm the 5-year presumption rule (IL 3 kap. 7 section, second paragraph): for Swedish citizens who emigrate, the burden of proof is on the taxpayer for 5 years after departure to show they lack vasentlig anknytning. The taxpayer's window runs until early 2028.

**Critical clarification (all 4 agree)**: This is an **evidentiary** rule, not a substantive tax rule. It does not make the taxpayer automatically obegransat skattskyldig -- it merely places the burden of proof on them if Skatteverket questions their status. The taxpayer's evidence package (no Swedish property/family/business, Polish JDG registration, Polish tax returns filed, Polish residence) is more than sufficient to rebut the presumption.

### None of the taxpayer's income is taxable in Sweden

All 4 sources analyze every income stream and reach the same conclusion: Sweden has zero taxing rights.

| Income Stream | Swedish Domestic Law | DTT Article | Taxable In |
|---|---|---|---|
| USDC salary (Byte Masons / Conclave) | Not Swedish-source (work in Poland, no Swedish employer) | Art. 14: taxable only where resident | **Poland only** |
| JDG ryczalt income (Clearstar EUR) | No Swedish PE | Art. 7: taxable only where enterprise resides | **Poland only** |
| Crypto trading (Binance/Kraken) | Not covered by 10-year rule (see below) | Art. 13(4): taxable only where alienator resides | **Poland only** |

**DTT reference**: Sweden-Poland Double Taxation Treaty, signed November 19, 2004, implemented as Swedish law via Lag (2005:248).

### The 10-year rule (tioarsregeln) does NOT apply to cryptocurrency

This is the single most important legal finding. All 4 sources confirm it unambiguously.

**The rule**: IL 3 kap. 19 section extends Swedish capital gains tax jurisdiction for up to 10 years after emigration -- but **only** for disposals of **delagarratter** (equity rights/shares as defined in IL 48 kap. 2 section) and certain partnership interests.

**Crypto classification**: HFD 2018 ref. 72 (the Swedish Supreme Administrative Court) definitively classified Bitcoin -- and by extension all comparable crypto -- as **ovriga tillgangar** ("other assets") under IL 52 kap. 3 section. This is confirmed by Skatteverket's published crypto guidance. Crypto is NOT a delagarratt, NOT a fordringsratt (debt instrument), and NOT a foreign currency.

**Consequence**: Because crypto falls under IL 52 kap. (other assets) and NOT under IL 48 kap. (equity rights), the 10-year rule in IL 3 kap. 19 section simply does not reach it. The DTT's Art. 13(5) 10-year clause is a permissive treaty provision ("may be taxed") but requires domestic enabling legislation to be operative -- and Sweden has none for crypto.

**Key references cited across sources**:
- HFD 2018 ref. 72 (mal nr 2674-18) -- Bitcoin classified as andra tillgangar
- Skatterattsnamnden Dnr 73-08/D (2009-04-02), affirmed by HFD mal nr 2662-09 (2010-12-14) -- 10-year rule scoped to delagarratter only
- Skatteverket crypto guidance page: kryptovalutor classified under 52 kap.

**Exception noted by all sources**: Swedish exchange-traded products (e.g., XBT Provider ETNs on Nasdaq Stockholm) ARE delagarratter and WOULD be covered by the 10-year rule. The taxpayer's holdings (BTC, ETH, DOT, USDC, USDT on Binance/Kraken) are native crypto assets and are not affected.

### No Swedish filing obligation exists if there is no Swedish-source income

All 4 sources confirm: a begransat skattskyldig person with zero Swedish-source income has **no statutory filing obligation** in Sweden. Skatteverket's own guidance for limited-tax-liable persons living abroad states they should NOT declare income from countries other than Sweden.

**Legal basis**: Skatteforfarandelagen (2011:1244) -- filing threshold requires Swedish-source income of at least 100 SEK (or 24,873 SEK for employment/business per Gemini); the taxpayer has 0 SEK Swedish-source income.

### DTT tie-breaker resolves decisively to Poland

All 4 sources confirm that even under a theoretical dual-residency scenario, the Sweden-Poland DTT Art. 4 tie-breaker resolves to Poland: permanent home in Poland, centre of vital interests in Poland, habitual abode in Poland. Swedish citizenship (the nationality backstop) is never reached.

## Strong Consensus (3-4 sources agree, minor nuance differences)

### Pre-filled returns: file "zero" with assertive note (recommended strategy)

All 4 sources address what to do if Skatteverket sends a pre-filled return (foritryckt sjalvdeklaration). The consensus strategy:

| Source | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| ChatGPT 5.4 Pro | File a short correction stating begransat skattskyldig status with no Swedish-source income, using Ovriga upplysningar |
| ChatGPT Deep Research | File a "zero Sweden-source income" return with clarifying note asserting limited tax liability |
| Perplexity | Submit the form with corrections asserting begransat skattskyldig status, reporting zero Swedish-source income, attach Polish tax residency certificate |
| Gemini | File "zero" return with a robust legal declaration in Ovriga upplysningar (provides exact Swedish-language draft text) |

**Do NOT ignore pre-filled returns**: All 4 sources warn that ignoring a pre-filled return risks late filing fees (forseningsavgift) and estimated assessment (skonsbeskattning). Even though the taxpayer has no filing obligation, if Skatteverket issues a return, the safest approach is to respond.

**Do NOT file worldwide income**: All 4 sources explicitly warn against filing full worldwide income to Sweden. This would incorrectly assert obegransat skattskyldighet, invite unnecessary Swedish processing, and potentially create double taxation complications. Gemini is particularly emphatic: this approach "misinterprets the function of the DTT" and "should be strictly avoided."

ChatGPT 5.4 Pro provides a concise Swedish-language template:

Jag ar obegransat skattskyldig i Polen och har min skatterattsliga hemvist dar enligt artikel 4 i skatteavtalet mellan Sverige och Polen (SFS 2005:248). Alla mina inkomster (inklusive utlandsk tjansteinkomst och kapitalvinster fran kryptovalutor) ar uteslutande skattepliktiga i Polen enligt avtalets artikel 7, 13 och 14. Kryptovalutor utgor 'ovriga tillgangar' (52 kap IL) varfor tioarsregeln (3 kap 19 section IL) ej ar tillamplig. Inga inkomster med kalla i Sverige har uppburits."

### Voluntary disclosure (sjalvrattelse) available for missed 2023/2024

All 4 sources confirm that Sweden has a voluntary correction mechanism analogous to Poland's czynny zal:

- **Name**: Sjalvrattelse pa eget initiativ (voluntary correction on own initiative)
- **Effect**: Eliminates skattetillagg (40% surcharge) and blocks criminal prosecution
- **Condition**: Must be filed before Skatteverket has initiated an investigation or sent a targeted inquiry
- **Window**: Corrections can go back 6 years (so in 2026, back to income year 2020)
- **Practical scope**: For this taxpayer, the "correction" is procedural -- filing zero-income returns with the legal assertion of limited tax liability

**Important nuance (Gemini)**: Sjalvrattelse does NOT waive forseningsavgift (late filing fees). If the 2023 return was due in May 2024 and was never filed, the maximum 3,750 SEK late fee has likely already accrued. This is a minor administrative cost, not a substantive tax risk.

### Penalties are capped and manageable

| Penalty | Amount | Applicability |
|---|---|---|
| Forseningsavgift (late filing fee) | 1,250 SEK initial + 1,250 after 3 months + 1,250 after 5 months = **max 3,750 SEK per year** | Applies if a return was issued and not filed on time. Unavoidable if late. |
| Skonsbeskattning (estimated assessment) | Unlimited -- based on Skatteverket's estimate of income | Applies if returns are ignored entirely. Skatteverket may use CRS data from exchanges to estimate crypto gains. **This is the dangerous one.** |
| Skattetillagg (tax surcharge) | **40% of hidden tax** | Only applies if there was actual underlying tax. Since the taxpayer's Swedish tax liability is 0 SEK, 40% of 0 = 0 SEK. Eliminated by sjalvrattelse. |

**Key insight (all sources agree)**: The skattetillagg is mathematically zero because the underlying Swedish tax liability is zero. The real risk is skonsbeskattning -- if Skatteverket estimates income based on CRS exchange data without the taxpayer's input, it could create a phantom tax bill that requires formal appeals to reverse.

### Register emigration immediately if not done

All 4 sources identify this as the highest-priority administrative step:

- **Form**: SKV 7665 / SKV 7665b (Anmalan - Flyttning fran Sverige)
- **Method**: Online via Skatteverket e-service (requires BankID) or paper form
- **Late filing**: There is no time limit. Filing late is better than not filing at all. Skatteverket updates the registry retroactively to the actual departure date.
- **Effect**: Deregisters from Folkbokforingen, aligns Sweden's records with reality, reduces administrative friction

All sources note that Folkbokforing (population registration) and tax residency are legally separate systems -- failure to deregister does not automatically make you tax-resident, but it creates administrative problems and makes Skatteverket's systems continue treating you as a resident.

### Obtain Polish certificate of tax residence (CFR-1)

All 4 sources recommend obtaining a Polish tax residency certificate:

- **Polish form**: CFR-1 (Certyfikat Rezydencji Podatkowej) / zaswiadczenie o miejscu zamieszkania dla celow podatkowych
- **Issued by**: Local Urzad Skarbowy
- **Purpose**: Documentary proof for Skatteverket that Poland claims the taxpayer as a tax resident under the DTT
- **Use**: Attach to any Swedish filing or correspondence, especially during the 5-year presumption window

### Forhandsbesked (advance ruling) is unnecessary

All 4 sources agree that applying for a forhandsbesked from Skatterattsnamnden is overkill for this case:

- Cost: ~6,000-20,000 SEK fee (Gemini estimates total legal costs at 20,000-50,000+ SEK)
- Timeline: 6-12 months
- Purpose: Intended for genuinely ambiguous situations
- This case: Straightforward -- no substantive ties to Sweden, clear DTT allocation

## Unique Insights by Source

### ChatGPT 5.4 Pro -- SRN 2026-04-09 precedent

Cites a very recent Skatterattsnamnden decision from April 9, 2026 where a Swedish sole proprietorship was the decisive factor keeping someone vasentligt anknyten to Sweden; once the sole proprietorship was deregistered, the connection ceased. Useful contrast: the taxpayer has NO Swedish business at all, making their case materially stronger.

### ChatGPT Deep Research -- CRS data risk

Uniquely emphasizes the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) angle: European crypto exchanges (Binance Europe, Kraken) report gross transaction volumes to tax authorities via CRS. If the taxpayer is still registered as a Swedish resident, this data may flow to Skatteverket, potentially triggering automated inquiries or skonsbeskattning based on gross volumes without cost basis offset.

### Perplexity -- DTT Art. 13(5) structural analysis

Provides the clearest structural argument for why the DTT's 10-year clause is inoperative for crypto: Art. 13(5) is a **permissive** clause ("may be taxed") that merely preserves treaty permission. It does not create domestic tax. Sweden still needs a domestic charging rule, and IL 3 kap. 19 section only charges delagarratter. Without domestic law, the treaty permission is empty.

Also uniquely flags **Dir. 2022:45** -- an ongoing Swedish government inquiry into taxing unrealized gains upon departure. As of April 2026, no legislation has been enacted, but this is worth monitoring for future changes.

### Gemini -- Swedish crypto calculation methodology

Provides the most detailed analysis of what would happen IF reporting were required (Section 6). Key differences from Polish rules:

- Sweden taxes **every** crypto disposal including crypto-to-crypto swaps (Poland does not)
- Sweden uses **genomsnittsmetoden** (average cost method), NOT FIFO or annual cost pooling
- K4 Bilaga D is the relevant form, requiring SEK values
- The taxpayer would need to recalculate the entire 2023-2025 transaction history under Swedish rules -- an enormous effort
- **This is all moot if the zero-filing strategy succeeds** (which all sources agree it should)

## Bottom Line

**All 4 sources unanimously agree**: The taxpayer is begransat skattskyldig in Sweden, has zero Swedish-source income, owes zero Swedish tax, and the 10-year rule does not apply to crypto. The only risk is administrative -- if Skatteverket has the taxpayer in the wrong bucket and has issued returns or estimated assessments. The fix is to (1) register the emigration, (2) file zero returns with a legal assertion, and (3) keep a Polish CFR-1 on hand. The total cost exposure is limited to forseningsavgift of up to ~3,750 SEK per missed year -- a trivial amount relative to the income and tax at stake.

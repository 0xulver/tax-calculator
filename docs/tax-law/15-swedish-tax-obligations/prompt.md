## Questions

### Residency status and filing obligation

1. Under Swedish law (Inkomstskattelagen 3 kap.), what is the taxpayer's Swedish tax status? Are they "begransat skattskyldig" (limited tax liability) or "obegransat skattskyldig" (unlimited tax liability)?

2. What is the "vasentlig anknytning" (essential connection) test (3 kap. 7 section IL)? List all the connection points evaluated. For someone with ONLY Swedish citizenship and possibly a bank account — no property, no family, no business, no real estate, no significant economic holdings in Sweden — do they have vasentlig anknytning?

3. There is a **5-year presumption** for Swedish citizens and long-term residents: during the 5 years after emigration, the burden of proof is on the taxpayer to show they lack vasentlig anknytning. Since the taxpayer moved in early 2023, they are within this window until early 2028. What does the taxpayer need to do or show to establish that they are begransat skattskyldig? Is registering an "utflyttning" (emigration) with Skatteverket required?

4. Does Swedish citizenship alone create vasentlig anknytning? What does case law say? What about having a Swedish bank account or personnummer?

### Filing requirements

5. If the taxpayer is begransat skattskyldig and has **zero Swedish-source income**: are they legally required to file a Swedish Inkomstdeklaration? Or is there no filing obligation?

6. Skatteverket may still send a pre-filled tax return (forifylld sjalvdeklaration). If the taxpayer receives one, are they required to respond? What happens if they ignore it?

7. What is the recommended approach: (a) not file at all, (b) file with zero income and a note asserting begransat skattskyldig status, or (c) file reporting worldwide income? What do Swedish tax advisors typically recommend for emigrants?

8. If the taxpayer has been ignoring Swedish tax returns for 2023 and 2024: what are the consequences? Penalties? Can this be corrected?

### What is taxable in Sweden for non-residents?

9. For a begransat skattskyldig person, what income is still taxable in Sweden? List the categories. Does any of the taxpayer's income (US client paying USDC, Polish JDG invoicing EUR, crypto trading on international exchanges) fall into any Swedish-taxable category?

10. Under the Sweden-Poland DTT (signed November 19, 2004):
    - Art. 7 (Business Profits): JDG income from Polish/international clients — taxable where?
    - Art. 14 (Independent Personal Services): freelance USDC income — taxable where?
    - Art. 13 (Capital Gains): crypto sold on international exchanges — taxable where?

IL). Reference Skatteverket's classification of crypto and any relevant forhandsbesked from Skatterattsnamnden.

### Consequences of different filing approaches

12. **Scenario A — Filing zero or not filing**: If the taxpayer is genuinely begransat skattskyldig with no Swedish-source income, is filing zero (or not filing) legal? Could Skatteverket challenge this? What would they need to prove?

13. **Scenario B — Filing accurately with full income**: If the taxpayer were to declare all worldwide income to Sweden as well, would double taxation result? How would the DTT prevent this? Would Sweden give credit for Polish taxes paid?

14. **Scenario C — Previously missed filings**: If the taxpayer should have filed for 2023 and 2024 but didn't, what is the correction process? Is there a Swedish equivalent of Poland's "czynny zal" (voluntary disclosure with reduced penalties)?

15. What are the penalties for:
    - Not filing when you should have (forseningsavgift)?
    - Filing incorrectly (skattetillagg)?
    - Is there a statute of limitations?

    - Is it sufficient to report total net income for the year converted to SEK?

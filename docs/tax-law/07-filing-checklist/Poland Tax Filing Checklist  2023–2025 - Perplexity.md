# Poland Tax Filing Checklist: 2023–2025
### For a Software Engineer with Crypto, Pre-JDG Income, and Ryczałt JDG

> **⚠️ Disclaimer**: This document is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal or tax advice. Given the complexity of your situation — especially the pre-JDG USDC income, late filings for 2023/2024, and the interplay of multiple income streams — consultation with a licensed Polish tax adviser (doradca podatkowy) is strongly recommended before filing.

***

## Executive Summary

As a Polish tax resident from approximately 2023, you have obligations across three distinct tax years. The core principle is that Poland taxes **worldwide income** on a self-reported basis. The most urgent action is filing late returns for 2023 and 2024 immediately — accompanied by *czynny żal* (active regret) letters to limit penalties — while also ensuring the 2025 return (due **April 30, 2026**) is filed on time. All crypto disposals, even at a net loss, require a PIT-38 filing. The USDC salary received before JDG establishment creates the most complex question in your situation and warrants specific professional guidance.

**Key rules for 2023**:
- PIT-38 must be filed even if you only purchased crypto but did not sell — the purchase costs are reported and carried forward.[^1][^2]
- If the **only** income source was crypto disposals, **PIT-38 is the sole required form** (plus PIT/ZG for foreign exchanges). There is no obligation to also file PIT-36 or PIT-37.[^2][^1]
- Crypto sold for EUR on Binance and Kraken: income and costs must be converted to PLN using the NBP average exchange rate on the last business day preceding each transaction.[^3][^4]
- The unregistered activity limit for 2023 was: **PLN 1,745 / month** (Jan–Jun: 50% of PLN 3,490 minimum wage) rising to **PLN 2,700 / month** (Jul–Dec: 75% of PLN 3,600). If any service income exceeded these monthly thresholds, it could not be treated as unregistered activity and would require PIT-36 reporting.[^5]

## Part 2: Detailed Question Answers

### Q1–2: Which Forms Are Required and How They Interact for 2025?

For 2025, the combination of three income streams requires three separate filings:[^12]

**PIT-38** covers only crypto capital gains/losses. It is calculated completely separately from other income — crypto gains cannot be offset against ordinary income losses, and vice versa. The net loss from 2025 crypto activity should still be reported so it can be carried forward for up to 5 years to offset future crypto gains.[^13][^14][^15][^1]

**PIT-28** covers JDG ryczałt income. IT services and programming are taxed at **12% ryczałt** on gross revenue. This is filed separately from PIT-36 and PIT-38, but PIT-28 and PIT-36 can be filed together in the same submission. Note that health insurance contributions are **not deductible** from income under ryczałt — they reduce neither the tax base nor the tax itself for ryczałt taxpayers.[^16][^17][^18][^19][^12]

**PIT-36** covers the pre-JDG USDC salary income. Since you performed work for a foreign entity without a registered Polish business, and the amounts received almost certainly exceeded the unregistered activity monthly threshold (40,500 USDC over 4 months ≈ ~10,125 USDC/month, far above the ~€800/month unregistered limit), this income must be reported on PIT-36 as income from "other sources" or as informal business income. Progressive tax rates apply: 0% on income up to PLN 30,000, 12% from PLN 30,001–120,000, and 32% above PLN 120,000. Currency conversion to PLN is required at the NBP rate for each payment date.[^20][^21][^22][^23][^24][^25][^3]

> ⚠️ **Important gray area**: The USDC salary income creates a significant classification question. Receiving regular, repeated payments for software services before registering a JDG may be treated by tax authorities as *de facto* economic activity that should have been registered. This matters because:
> 1. It could affect ZUS obligations (see Part 3)
> 2. It may be treated as undeclared business income rather than "other sources"
> 3. It could trigger interest and penalties on any unpaid advance payments
> A tax adviser should confirm the correct PIT-36 treatment.

***

### Q3: Must PIT-38 Be Filed Even With a Net Loss?

**Yes, unambiguously.** Polish tax law does not exempt loss-making years from the PIT-38 filing obligation. Filing the loss is essential to:[^26][^14][^15]
- Preserve the right to carry the loss forward (for up to 5 years)
- Offset future crypto gains within the statutory limits
- Document the acquisition costs of any unsold crypto

Furthermore, a loss shown in EUR or USD does not automatically mean a loss in PLN — exchange rate fluctuations can change the PLN result significantly, and the tax authority calculates in PLN.[^26]

***

### Q4: If Only Crypto Income in 2023 — Is PIT-38 the Only Form?

**Yes.** If the only income in 2023 was from crypto disposals (selling for EUR on Binance/Kraken), then **PIT-38 plus PIT/ZG** are the only required forms. There is no obligation to file PIT-36 or PIT-37 for a year with only capital gains income. The key condition: there must genuinely be no other income sources (service income, employment, dividends, etc.).[^27][^1][^2]

### Q5–6: VAT Obligations

| Activity | VAT Treatment | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| **Selling crypto for fiat (EUR)** | ✅ VAT-**EXEMPT** | Under CJEU Hedqvist ruling (C-264/14), exchanging virtual currencies for fiat is treated as a financial service and exempt from VAT[^28][^29][^30]. Binding in Poland. |
| **Receiving USDC as payment for services** | ⚠️ The **service** may be VATable; USDC as payment medium is not | The service you provided is the VATable supply. The fact it was paid in USDC is not a separate VAT event, but the invoice must convert the amount to PLN at the NBP rate[^27][^3]. |
| **JDG IT services to EU B2B clients** | ✅ **Reverse charge** — no Polish VAT | For B2B services to EU-registered businesses, the place of taxation is the customer's country, and the customer accounts for VAT under reverse charge. You issue an invoice without VAT, noting "reverse charge"[^31][^32]. |
| **VAT registration threshold** | PLN 240,000 (~€57k) in 2026 | If annual taxable revenue stays below this, you may operate as VAT-exempt[^33]. |

**Conclusion**: Crypto trading is VAT-exempt. For IT services to foreign B2B clients, you do not charge Polish VAT. You likely do not need to register for VAT unless domestic taxable revenues exceed PLN 240,000.

## Part 3: Social Insurance (ZUS)

### Q7–8: ZUS Obligations from USDC Salary Before JDG

This is the most legally uncertain area of your situation. The answer depends on how the pre-JDG USDC income was structured:

| Scenario | ZUS Obligation |
|----------|----------------|
| **Unregistered activity** (działalność nierejestrowana) | No ZUS, but only valid if monthly revenue stayed below the monthly limit (~€800) AND no prior business in last 60 months[^20][^34] — 40,500 USDC over 4 months clearly exceeds this |
| **Umowa zlecenie / contract of mandate** with foreign entity | ZUS should be paid by the payer (the foreign client) as the "płatnik." In practice, foreign companies often don't fulfill this obligation for Polish residents[^7] |
| **B2B without registration (de facto economic activity)** | Should have been registered in CEIDG. May be treated retrospectively as informal business activity, creating retroactive ZUS obligations |
| **Employment contract (umowa o pracę)** | Employer withholds and pays ZUS — but no formal employment was described |

**Practical implication**: If the USDC payments were treated as informal freelance income without any formal contract structure, and the amounts exceeded the unregistered activity limits, there is a real risk of retroactive ZUS obligations. However, if the payments were structured as a services/B2B arrangement with a foreign company, and the foreign company was the payer/employer, the ZUS burden shifts to them (even if they didn't pay it in practice).

> **Strong recommendation**: Discuss the specific contractual arrangement with a ZUS specialist or tax adviser to determine whether any retroactive ZUS registration and payment is required. The cost of not addressing this can accumulate significantly over time.

### Q9: JDG ZUS — From Start Date or Retroactively?

ZUS contributions for JDG begin from the **date of CEIDG registration**, not retroactively. Within 7 days of CEIDG registration, you must register with ZUS by submitting form **ZUS ZUA** (full social insurance) or **ZUS ZZA** (health insurance only if eligible for "Ulga na Start").[^33][^35]

**New JDG relief structure (if this was the first business registration in 60+ months)**:
| Period | ZUS Regime | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| First 6 calendar months | **Ulga na start** — no social contributions | Pay only health insurance contribution[^35][^36] |
| Months 7–30 | **Preferential ZUS** — base = 30% of minimum wage | In 2025: 30% × PLN 4,666 = PLN 1,399.80 base[^36] |
| After month 30 | **Full ZUS** | Base = 60% of projected average wage |

## Part 4: Health Insurance (NFZ/Składka Zdrowotna)

### Q10–12: Health Insurance for Ryczałt JDG

For a JDG taxed under ryczałt, the monthly health insurance contribution in 2025 is calculated as a **fixed amount based on annual revenue tier** — not as a percentage of actual income:[^37][^38][^39]

| Annual Revenue | Monthly Health Contribution (2025) |
|----------------|--------------------------------------|
| Up to PLN 60,000 | ~PLN 461/month (9% × 60% × PLN 8,549.18)[^39] |
| PLN 60,001–300,000 | ~PLN 769/month (9% × 100% × PLN 8,549.18)[^39] |
| Above PLN 300,000 | ~PLN 1,384/month (9% × 180% × PLN 8,549.18)[^39] |

**Is the health contribution deductible?** No. For ryczałt taxpayers, the health insurance contribution is **not deductible** from income or from the tax due. This is a significant cost.[^17][^18]

**Annual health insurance settlement for ryczałt**: Filed as part of the **ZUS DRA declaration for April of the following year** (April DRA submitted by May 20). This finalizes the contribution based on actual annual revenue.[^40][^41]

**Before JDG (Jan–Apr 2025)**: If there was no employment contract, no JDG, and no other ZUS-covered basis, there was technically no mandatory health insurance coverage under NFZ. However, this creates a coverage gap. This is a common issue for early-stage freelancers and is generally addressed retroactively once JDG is established — JDG coverage starts from registration date.

## Part 6: Late Filing — Rules and Remedies

### Czynny Żal (Active Regret)

For the missed 2023 and 2024 filings, the primary legal mechanism to avoid criminal tax penalties is **czynny żal** (Art. 16 of the Fiscal Penal Code).[^44][^45][^46]

**How it works**: Submit a written notification to the relevant tax office simultaneously with (or immediately before) filing the late return. The notice must:
- Acknowledge the failure to file on time
- Describe the circumstances
- Demonstrate willingness to comply going forward
- Accompany the actual late return and payment of any tax owed (plus interest)[^46][^42]

**Critical timing**: Czynny żal is only effective **before** the tax authority independently discovers the offense or begins verification proceedings. Given that Poland signed the DAC8 law in March 2026 (data exchange starting 2027 for 2026 data), there is currently a window where 2023/2024 crypto data has not yet been automatically shared — but this will change. Act immediately.[^47][^46]

**Penalty structure for late filing**:[^48]
| Delay | Typical Penalty |
|-------|-----------------|
| Under 30 days | ~PLN 200 |
| Over 30 days | PLN 500–1,000 per form |
| Deliberate evasion (large amounts) | Criminal prosecution, up to 20× minimum wage |

**Interest on unpaid tax**: Calculated daily from the original deadline. Standard interest rate is set by the Ministry of Finance (typically 8–15% annually). Use the ZUS interest calculator at kalkulatory.gofin.pl.[^42]

## Part 8: The USDC Stablecoin Question — Special Analysis

USDC (USD Coin) presents a nuanced tax situation in Poland:

**As a means of payment** (receiving USDC as compensation for services):
- USDC received as payment for services is treated as income at the moment of receipt, valued at market rate (approximately 1:1 with USD, converted to PLN at NBP rate)[^3]
- The income from the service is subject to PIT (on PIT-36 if personal, or as JDG income)
- Receiving USDC is not itself a separate crypto taxable event — you are simply receiving payment

**When you later sell USDC for EUR**:
- USDC is treated as a "virtual currency" under Polish law (it meets the definition: digital representation of value exchangeable for legal tender)[^52]
- Exchanging USDC for EUR is a **taxable disposal** reported on PIT-38 at 19%[^53][^27]
- Your cost basis for the USDC is the PLN value at the time you received it as service payment
- If you received 40,500 USDC at ~PLN 4/USDC = PLN 162,000 basis, and later sold them for equivalent EUR, the gain or loss is the difference

**USDC → USDT or USDC → DOT swaps**: Crypto-to-crypto is **not taxable**. Only the final conversion to fiat triggers PIT-38.[^54][^55]

* **2024:** again **PIT-38** for crypto; add **PIT-28 / PIT-36 / PIT-36L / PIT-37** only if 2024 actually had JDG or other non-crypto income. ([Podatki][2])
* **2025:** on the facts described, the working filing package is **PIT-28** (registered JDG on ryczałt) **+ PIT-38** (crypto disposals/cost carryforward) and **likely PIT-36** for the **Jan–Apr 2025 pre-JDG service income paid in USDC**, unless those Jan–Apr payments were already reported through a Polish payer or are reclassified as business income from an earlier factual business start. **PIT-36L is not the right form** for a ryczałt JDG. ([Podatki][1])
* **Yes, PIT-38 must be filed even with no tax due.** MF says PIT-38 is required if you had crypto disposal **or even just acquisition costs**, and for crypto the law **does not recognize a normal tax “loss”**; excess costs are carried forward to later years instead. ([Podatki][2])

## 2025: how the forms interact

The cleanest working model for 2025 is:

1. **Jan–Apr 2025 service income paid in USDC** → ordinary service/work income, **not** PIT-38 merely because it was paid in crypto. MF’s contract-income page says the income from such contracts is the money/monetary value received and also the value of benefits in kind. ([Podatki][4])
2. **Later sale of that received USDC for EUR** (or using it to pay for goods/services) → **PIT-38 crypto disposal**. MF states crypto disposal includes exchange for legal tender, goods, services, other property rights, or settling liabilities with crypto. ([Podatki][2])
3. **Post-registration JDG invoices taxed by ryczałt** → **PIT-28**, not PIT-36L. ([Podatki][1])

So, on the facts described, **2025 is very likely a multi-return year**: **PIT-28 + PIT-38 + probably PIT-36**. The main legal uncertainty is whether Jan–Apr 2025 should stay outside the JDG or be reclassified as earlier factual business activity. ([Podatki][1])

## Direct answers to your numbered questions

### 1–4. Which forms must be filed?

* **PIT-38**: yes for every year in which you had taxable crypto disposals or crypto acquisition costs to report/carry forward. MF says PIT-38 is required if you had crypto disposal or acquisition costs, even when no sale occurred that year. ([Podatki][2])
* **PIT-28**: only for years in which a JDG was taxed by **ryczałt**. On your facts that is **2025**, and possibly **2024** if a JDG really existed then and used ryczałt. ([Podatki][1])
* **PIT-36L**: only for years in which a JDG was taxed by **linear 19%**. On the facts given, **not 2025**. It is only a possible 2024 form if 2024 JDG existed and was on linear tax. ([Podatki][6])
* **PIT-36**: for scale-taxed business or for certain income without a Polish payer / foreign-source work or service income. For your 2025 pre-JDG USDC service income, **PIT-36 is the likely working assumption** unless there was a Polish payer or the months are reclassified into the JDG. ([Podatki][3])
* **PIT-37**: only if you had employment or civil-contract income settled **through a payer**. It does **not** replace PIT-38 for crypto or PIT-28 for ryczałt business. ([Podatki][7])
* **PIT/ZG**: only if you report **foreign-source income** in the annual return; for PIT-36 it is one annex per country, and MF also confirms PIT/ZG can attach to PIT-38 when foreign capital income is reported. ([Podatki][3])
* **PIT-16A**: not on your facts, unless some year was actually on **karta podatkowa**. ([Podatki][8])

### 5–6. VAT

For **private crypto investing/trading**, your facts do not point to a separate Polish VAT filing. Where crypto exchange is treated as a VAT supply, Polish MF interpretations have accepted VAT exemption for bitcoin sales under **art. 43 ust. 1 pkt 7**, and EU VAT guidance following **Hedqvist** treats exchange of bitcoin for fiat or other crypto as **taxable but exempt** under the VAT Directive. ([Eureka][9])

For **IT services to EU business clients**, Biznes.gov.pl says the service **as a rule is not taxed in Poland**, and the invoice should be issued **without Polish VAT** with a **reverse-charge** note. VAT-UE information is filed electronically by the **25th day of the following month** when that obligation arises. If you are an active VAT taxpayer, **JPK_V7** is also filed monthly by the **25th day**. ([Business.gov.pl][10])

So the VAT question for the Jan–Apr 2025 USDC payments is **about the service**, not about the token. The unresolved point is whether those months were already a VAT-taxable economic activity in substance, despite the JDG not yet being formally registered.

### 7–9. ZUS

ZUS follows the **legal title**, not the payment medium. ZUS says a **zlecenie** creates mandatory social insurance from the contractual start date until the end of the contract, while a **JDG** creates mandatory social insurance from the start of the activity until it ends; for JDG, sickness insurance is voluntary. ([zus.pl][11])

That means **mere receipt of USDC is not itself a standalone ZUS title**. If Jan–Apr 2025 truly had **no employment, no zlecenie, and no JDG**, there is no normal “entrepreneur ZUS” just because money came in. But that does **not** make it “działalność nierejestrowana”: Biznes.gov.pl says unregistered activity requires monthly revenue not exceeding **75% of minimum wage**, and that regime has no ZUS or health contributions. Your Jan–Apr 2025 amount is far above that ceiling, so those months need to be classified as some other legal title or as de facto earlier business activity. ([Business.gov.pl][12])

Once a **JDG** exists, ZUS runs from the **actual start of the activity**. So if the factual business start equals the CEIDG start date, ZUS starts there. If the authorities conclude that the factual business started earlier, there is retroactive risk from that earlier date. ([zus.pl][11])

If the 2025 JDG was your first one (or restarted after the required break) and you met the other conditions, **ulga na start** may have been available, meaning only health insurance for the first 6 months instead of social contributions. ([Business.gov.pl][13])

### 10–12. Health insurance / NFZ

For a **ryczałt JDG in 2025**, ZUS set three monthly health-contribution bands based on cumulative annual revenue:

* up to **60,000 PLN** revenue: base **5,129.51 PLN**, contribution **461.66 PLN**;
* over **60,000 PLN** up to **300,000 PLN**: base **8,549.18 PLN**, contribution **769.43 PLN**;
* over **300,000 PLN**: base **15,388.52 PLN**, contribution **1,384.97 PLN**. ([zus.pl][14])

For **ryczałt**, MF says you may deduct **50% of paid health contributions** from revenue. ([Podatki][15])

Annual health reconciliation is done in the **April ZUS DRA/RCA of the following year**. ZUS’s general guidance says the annual reconciliation is filed in the April document, and its 2024 announcement says the **2024** reconciliation had to be included in the **April 2025** document due **20 May 2025**. On that pattern, any **2025 JDG** reconciliation belongs in the **April 2026** document due **20 May 2026**. ([zus.pl][16])

For the **months before JDG**, there is no JDG health contribution. Those months would have needed some other health-insurance title or voluntary coverage.

([Podatki][21])

### 16–18. International obligations

MF states that a Polish tax resident has **unlimited tax liability** in Poland and reports worldwide income here, subject to treaty rules. ([Podatki][22])

**PIT/ZG** is required when you report **foreign-source income** in the annual return; for PIT-36, MF says it is filed **separately for each country**. For PIT-38, MF also says to attach PIT/ZG if you had capital income obtained abroad. ([Podatki][3])

I did **not** find any annual Polish PIT annex that is filed **solely because you hold** a foreign bank account, a foreign exchange account, or a foreign crypto wallet. The official MF CRS page instead describes **institution-to-KAS reporting** of financial accounts and notes that Poland also receives information on Polish residents’ accounts held abroad. ([Podatki][23])

So, for **CRS/FATCA**, the main implication is usually **institutional reporting**, not an extra PIT form you personally file. Whether a specific exchange account is reportable under a given regime depends on that institution and regime.

1. **2023**

   * File/check **PIT-38**.
   * Add **PIT-36 or PIT-37 only if** 2023 had non-crypto income.
   * Add **PIT/ZG only if** some of that income was foreign-source.

2. **2024**

   * File/check **PIT-38**.
   * Determine whether any JDG actually existed, and if so whether it was **ryczałt / skala / liniowy**.
   * Determine whether any non-business service income existed.

3. **2025**

   * **PIT-28** for the ryczałt JDG.
   * **PIT-38** for all crypto disposals and cost carryforward.
   * **Likely PIT-36** for Jan–Apr 2025 USDC service income unless a document trail proves a different treatment.
   * **PIT/ZG** only if some PIT-36/PIT-38 income is actually foreign-source.

The fastest way to lock this down is to classify every 2023–2025 inflow into one of four buckets: **crypto disposal**, **payer-based employment/contract income**, **no-payer personal/service income**, or **JDG business income**. Once that bucket list exists, the form set is mostly mechanical.

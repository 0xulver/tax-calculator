# Research: Tax Classification of Stablecoins (USDC, USDT) in Poland

## Context

A Polish taxpayer regularly uses stablecoins (USDC, USDT) as an intermediary between crypto and fiat. The typical flow is:

1. Receive USDC as salary payment on Polygon
2. Transfer USDC to Binance or Kraken exchange
3. Convert USDC to EUR on the exchange
4. Withdraw EUR to bank account

Also common:
- Receive USDT from selling other crypto (e.g., sell ETH for USDT on Binance)
- Convert USDT to USDC via Binance Convert
- Then convert USDC to EUR

For tax calculations, the classification of stablecoins matters enormously:
- If USDC/USDT are "waluta wirtualna" (virtual currency): crypto-to-stablecoin swaps are non-taxable, and selling stablecoins for fiat is the taxable event
- If USDC/USDT are treated like USD equivalents: every trade through them could be treated differently

## Questions

### Legal Classification
1. Are USDC and USDT legally classified as "waluta wirtualna" (virtual currency) under Polish law (Ustawa o przeciwdzialaniu praniu pieniedzy, Art. 2 ust. 2 pkt 26)?

2. Has the Director of KIS (Krajowa Informacja Skarbowa) issued any individual interpretations (interpretacje indywidualne) specifically about stablecoins? If so, what do they say?

3. Does the EU MiCA regulation (Markets in Crypto-Assets), which came into force in 2024, change the classification of stablecoins in Poland? MiCA distinguishes between "e-money tokens" (like USDC) and other crypto-assets.

4. Under MiCA, USDC is classified as an "e-money token" (Art. 3(1)(7) MiCAR). Does this change its Polish tax treatment starting from any specific date?

### Tax Implications of Classification
5. If USDC is a "waluta wirtualna": converting ETH to USDC is NOT taxable (crypto-to-crypto), and converting USDC to EUR IS taxable. Correct?

6. If USDC were reclassified under MiCA as something other than "waluta wirtualna": would converting ETH to USDC become a taxable event (like selling for fiat)?

7. When converting USDC to EUR, what is the "revenue" for tax purposes? Is it:
   - The EUR amount received, converted to PLN at NBP rate?
   - The USDC amount times NBP USD rate?
   - Something else?

8. What is the cost basis of USDC that was:
   a) Received as salary payment -- original PLN value at receipt?
   b) Received from selling other crypto (ETH -> USDC swap) -- inherits cost basis of the sold crypto?
   c) Purchased directly with EUR -- EUR amount at NBP rate?

### USDC-to-USDT and Stablecoin-to-Stablecoin Swaps
9. Is converting USDC to USDT (or vice versa) a taxable event? Or is it crypto-to-crypto (non-taxable)?
10. When Binance auto-converted BUSD to FDUSD to USDC (stablecoin auto-conversions), is any of that taxable?

### Practical Impact
11. If the taxpayer converts 6,000 USDC (received as salary, valued at 24,000 PLN) to 5,500 EUR (worth 23,500 PLN at the time), is the taxable event:
    a) A crypto disposal with revenue 23,500 PLN, cost basis 24,000 PLN, loss of 500 PLN?
    b) No event because stablecoins aren't really crypto?
    c) Something else?

## Desired Output

Clear classification of stablecoins under Polish law with legal references, any KIS interpretations, impact of MiCA, and practical examples showing how USDC/USDT transactions should be reported.

# Research: Tax Treatment of DeFi, Staking, and Airdrops in Poland

## Context

The taxpayer has engaged in various crypto activities beyond simple buy/sell:

1. **Staking rewards**: DOT staking on Kraken (weekly rewards), ETH staking, SOL staking, and others (ATOM, XTZ, FLOW, KAVA, MINA, TRX, ADA)
2. **Earn/lending rewards**: Kraken earn program, Binance Simple Earn
3. **Airdrops**: Received various airdrop tokens on Binance
4. **DeFi activities**: Participated in Terra/Anchor protocol (before collapse), Polkadot ecosystem DeFi, possibly EVM DeFi
5. **Crowdloans**: Kusama parachain crowdloans (locking KSM to support parachain bids, receiving project tokens)
6. **Token swaps/redenomination**: e.g., LUNA to LUNA2 after Terra collapse
7. **Stablecoin auto-conversions**: Binance BUSD -> FDUSD -> USDC forced conversions

## Questions

### Staking Rewards
1. When DOT staking reward is received on Kraken, is this:
   a) Income taxable at receipt (like interest income)?
   b) A new crypto acquisition with zero cost basis (taxable only at disposal)?
   c) Not a taxable event at all until sold for fiat?

2. The law references "nabycie walut wirtualnych" -- does receiving staking rewards count as "acquisition" (nabycie)?

3. If staking rewards are taxed at receipt: what form? PIT-38 or PIT-36?

4. If not taxed at receipt: what is the cost basis when the staking reward tokens are eventually sold?

5. For liquid staking tokens (like stETH, DOT.S): is receiving them in exchange for staking the underlying asset a taxable event?

### Airdrops
6. How are airdrops classified under Polish tax law?
7. If an airdrop token has value at receipt: is the receipt itself taxable?
8. If an airdrop token has no market value at receipt (worthless token): cost basis is zero?
9. When the airdropped token is later sold for fiat, the entire proceeds are gain?

### Earn/Lending Programs
10. Binance Simple Earn and Kraken Earn distribute rewards periodically. Same questions as staking:
    - Taxable at receipt or only at disposal?
    - What cost basis?
    - What form to report?

### Crowdloans (Kusama/Polkadot)
11. In a Kusama crowdloan: KSM is locked and returned after the lease period. Project tokens are received as a reward. Tax treatment?
    - Locking KSM: not a disposal (it's returned)?
    - Receiving project tokens: income at receipt? Or crypto-to-crypto?
    - KSM returned after lease: no tax event?

### Token Swaps / Redenomination
12. When LUNA collapsed and became LUNA2 through a chain fork/migration:
    - Is receiving LUNA2 tokens a taxable event?
    - What is the cost basis of LUNA2?
    - What happens to the old LUNA tokens in FIFO?

13. When Binance auto-converted BUSD to FDUSD and then FDUSD to USDC:
    - Are these taxable events?
    - How should cost basis transfer?

### Terra/Anchor Collapse
14. The taxpayer likely had UST (algorithmic stablecoin) and LUNA that went to near-zero:
    - Can these losses be claimed?
    - Is the remaining dust in LUNA2 tokens relevant?

### Practical FIFO Impact
15. For staking rewards received over many months: each receipt creates a new FIFO lot?
16. If hundreds of small staking rewards exist, must each be individually valued in PLN?
17. Is there a materiality threshold or simplified method for small amounts?

## Desired Output

Classification of each activity type with:
- When taxable (receipt vs disposal)
- Tax form (PIT-38 vs PIT-36)
- Cost basis determination
- FIFO treatment
- Legal references and any KIS interpretations

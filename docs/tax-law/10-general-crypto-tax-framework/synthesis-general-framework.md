## Complete Consensus

### The core regime (all 4 agree perfectly)
- **19% flat tax** on "odplatne zbycie walut wirtualnych" (Art. 30b ust. 1a)
- Reported on **PIT-38, Section E** — ring-fenced from all other income
- **Crypto-to-crypto swaps are NOT taxable** (since 2019 reform)
- Costs = documented direct acquisition expenses + disposal fees (Art. 22 ust. 14)
- Excess costs carry forward indefinitely (Art. 22 ust. 16) — NOT a traditional "loss"
- Annual cost pool method — not per-transaction lot matching
- Must file PIT-38 even with zero revenue (to preserve cost carry-forward)
- Definition of "waluta wirtualna" imported from AML Act Art. 2 ust. 2 pkt 26
- Regime took effect **January 1, 2019** (replacing chaotic pre-2019 treatment)
- No separate crypto tax act — everything lives in the PIT Act

### What's clearly taxable vs non-taxable

| Event | Taxable? | All agree? |
|---|---|---|
| Crypto -> fiat (EUR, PLN) | Yes (19%) | Yes |
| Crypto -> goods/services | Yes (19%) | Yes |
| Crypto -> settle debts | Yes (19%) | Yes |
| Crypto -> crypto swap | No | Yes |
| Buying crypto with fiat | No (creates cost) | Yes |
| Wallet transfers | No | Yes |
| Holding crypto | No | Yes |
| Gift (for donor) | No PIT (may trigger gift tax for recipient) | Yes |

### JDG and crypto trading
All 4 confirm: a JDG that merely trades its own crypto portfolio uses **PIT-38**, not PIT-36L/PIT-28. Art. 17 ust. 1g explicitly keeps crypto in the capital gains bucket even within business activity. The ONLY exception is actual regulated crypto-service businesses (exchanges, intermediaries) under AML Art. 2 ust. 1 pkt 12.

### DAC8 — the game-changer
All sources emphasize this as the most significant near-term development:
- Signed into Polish law **March 9, 2026**
- Data collection from exchanges starts **January 1, 2026**
- User self-certification deadline: **October 31, 2026**
- Account suspension for non-certifiers: **December 31, 2026**
- First data transmission to KAS: **June 30, 2027**
- EU inter-state exchange: **September 30, 2027**
- Covers Binance, Kraken, and all EU-licensed CASPs
- Up to **75% punitive tax** on unreported crypto income discovered in audit

### MiCA — market regulation, not tax
MiCA does not change PIT rates or crypto tax rules. It regulates service providers. Poland's implementing law (ustawa o rynku kryptoaktywow) has been **vetoed twice** (Dec 2025, Feb 2026). Third attempt pending. Deadline: July 1, 2026.

### No crypto-specific amnesty
No dedicated crypto amnesty program. General mechanisms (czynny zal, korekta) apply.

## Interesting Details Only Some Sources Provide

### Pre-2019 regime (Perplexity/Gemini add historical context)
Before 2019, crypto was catastrophically taxed:
- Classified as property rights (Art. 18) at progressive rates (18%/32%)
- Crypto-to-crypto was taxable **barter** with 1% PCC on gross value per transaction
- Active traders could owe tax exceeding their profits
- MF issued temporary PCC waiver (July 2018 - June 2019)
- NSA confirmed this approach in II FSK 488/16 (March 2018)

The 2019 reform fixed all of this. Important for understanding why the current system works the way it does.

### NFTs are NOT "waluta wirtualna" (ChatGPT 5.4 Pro, Gemini, Perplexity)
NFTs are non-fungible and don't meet the "accepted as medium of exchange" criterion. They're classified as property rights (prawa majatkowe), taxed at progressive 12%/32% on PIT-36, not the favorable 19% crypto rate. Exchanging crypto for an NFT IS a taxable disposal of the crypto (exchange for a non-crypto property right).

### LP tokens are the riskiest DeFi item (all sources)
LP tokens from AMMs (Uniswap, etc.) may not qualify as "waluta wirtualna" — they look more like claims/participation rights. If classified as non-crypto property rights, depositing crypto to receive an LP token could be a taxable disposal. This is the largest unresolved DeFi risk area.

### The solidarity surcharge (Perplexity/Gemini)
A 4% additional tax (danina solidarnosciowa) applies when **total income from all sources** (including crypto) exceeds 1,000,000 PLN in a year. Filed on DSF-1. Not relevant for your situation unless gains become very large.

### Mining cost basis is 0 PLN (all sources)
Mining hardware and electricity are NOT deductible under Art. 22 ust. 14 (confirmed by NSA). Mined coins have zero cost basis. When later sold, the full amount is taxable income. This was confirmed by the Supreme Administrative Court.

### STIR monitoring system (Gemini)
KAS uses the STIR (System Teleinformatyczny Izby Rozliczeniowej) automated monitoring system that flags bank transactions over EUR 15,000 and cross-references them against tax declarations. Large fiat withdrawals from exchanges are a primary audit trigger even before DAC8.

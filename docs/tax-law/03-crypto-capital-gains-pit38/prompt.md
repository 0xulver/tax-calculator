# Research: Crypto Capital Gains and PIT-38 in Poland

## Context

A Polish tax resident has been trading and holding cryptocurrency since 2020 across Binance and Kraken exchanges, plus self-custody wallets on Bitcoin, Ethereum, Polkadot, Kusama, Solana, and Sui chains. They became a Polish tax resident around 2023 (previously Sweden). They need to understand the exact rules for PIT-38 crypto tax reporting for tax years 2023, 2024, and 2025.

Activities include:
- Buying crypto with EUR (fiat)
- Selling crypto for EUR (fiat) -- the main taxable events
- Crypto-to-crypto swaps (e.g., BTC to ETH, USDC to USDT)
- Receiving staking rewards (DOT, ETH, SOL, etc.)
- Receiving airdrops
- Earning interest on DeFi/CeFi platforms
- Losing funds on collapsed platforms (Celsius, BlockFi, Terra/UST)
- Depositing/withdrawing between exchanges and self-custody wallets

## Questions

### Taxable vs Non-Taxable Events
1. Confirm: under Polish law (Art. 17 ust. 1 pkt 11, Art. 30b ustawy o PIT), only "odplatne zbycie walut wirtualnych" (paid disposal of virtual currencies) is taxable at 19%. Crypto-to-crypto swaps are NOT taxable (this changed in 2019 -- confirm the current rule).

2. What exactly constitutes "odplatne zbycie" (paid disposal)?
   - Selling crypto for fiat (EUR, PLN) on an exchange -- taxable?
   - Converting USDC to EUR via Binance Convert -- taxable?
   - Converting USDT to USDC (stablecoin to stablecoin) -- taxable?
   - Selling crypto for goods/services directly -- taxable?
   - Sending crypto as a gift -- taxable?

3. Are staking rewards taxable at receipt, at disposal, or not at all? What is the cost basis of staking rewards?

4. Are airdrops taxable at receipt? What cost basis do they get?

5. Is earning interest on crypto (e.g., Binance Simple Earn) a taxable event at receipt?

6. If crypto is lost due to platform collapse (Celsius, BlockFi), can the loss be claimed? How?

### Cost Basis and FIFO
7. Confirm that Poland requires FIFO (First In, First Out) for cost basis calculation. Is this explicitly stated in the law? Reference?

8. For cost basis, what exchange rate must be used for EUR-denominated purchases? Is it the NBP mid-rate from the last business day BEFORE the transaction (as per Art. 11a ust. 2 ustawy o PIT)?

9. Does the cost basis include exchange trading fees? What about withdrawal fees? Network gas fees?

10. If crypto was acquired before becoming a Polish tax resident (e.g., bought BTC in 2021 while in Sweden), what cost basis applies? The original acquisition cost in PLN equivalent?

11. Must FIFO be applied per-asset (each cryptocurrency separately) or across all crypto?

### PIT-38 Form Details
12. What specific fields on PIT-38 relate to crypto? Is it Section E (przychody z odpłatnego zbycia walut wirtualnych)?

13. What needs to be reported:
    - Total revenue (przychod) from all crypto disposals in the year?
    - Total cost basis (koszty uzyskania przychodu)?
    - Net income or loss (dochod/strata)?
    - Individual transactions, or just totals?

14. Are crypto gains combined with other capital gains (stocks, etc.) on PIT-38, or are they a separate category?

15. What is the filing deadline for PIT-38? Is it April 30 of the following year?

### Documentation Requirements
16. What records must be kept to support a PIT-38 filing?
17. Must the taxpayer maintain a "ewidencja walut wirtualnych" (virtual currency register)?
18. What does Art. 30b ust. 6 ustawy o PIT require in terms of record-keeping?
19. Are exchange statements (Binance/Kraken CSV exports) sufficient, or is additional documentation needed?

## Desired Output

A comprehensive guide to PIT-38 crypto tax reporting in Poland with:
- Exact legal references (article numbers from ustawa o PIT)
- Clear taxable/non-taxable classification for each event type
- Step-by-step PIT-38 form filling instructions for crypto
- Documentation requirements
